Mission Running · Security Agents

Mission Runner's Handbook

A complete guide to ISK, LP, and standing — from your first L1 to blitzing L4s in a Marauder.

~20 min read All skill levels High-sec & Low-sec

What Are Missions?

Security missions are PvE assignments given by NPC agents. You fly to a deadspace pocket, destroy the enemy forces (and sometimes complete an objective like destroying a specific structure or picking up a cargo item), and return to collect your reward — ISK, Loyalty Points, and standings.

Missions are the most accessible ISK-making activity in EVE, available from day one, and scalable all the way up to Marauder-level income that competes with null-sec ratting. They also build faction standings that unlock better agents, faction store access, and reduced market taxes in station.

Key Insight: Bounties and mission rewards are just the appetizer. Loyalty Points (LP) are often worth 3-5× the cash reward and are where the real income lives — especially if you pick the right faction to grind.

NPC Faction Damage Reference

Every pirate faction deals a specific mix of damage types and has corresponding resistance holes. Knowing this lets you fit the right tank and the right ammo — it's the single biggest efficiency multiplier in mission running.

The four damage types are EM Thermal Kinetic Explosive. Match your tank resists to what the enemy shoots, and use the ammo type that hits their lowest resistance.

Tank Types

Your tank determines how long you survive in a mission pocket. The goal is to stack resistance to the faction's specific damage types, not just raw HP. A 60,000 EHP shield with the right resists beats a 90,000 EHP buffer with the wrong ones.

Shield Tank

Uses mid slots for Shield Extenders, Hardeners, and Boost Amplifiers. Shields regenerate passively over time, making passive shield builds possible (Drake, Rattlesnake, Golem). Active shield tanking uses Shield Boosters powered by cap. Shields have higher natural resistance to EM than armor but weaker against Explosive — choose shield when facing Guristas, Serpentis, or Mordu.

Armor Tank

Uses low slots for Armor Plating, Armor Hardeners, and Armor Repairers. Armor has higher base HP and better natural resistance to Explosive, but repairs consume significant capacitor. Armor is preferred against EM/Thermal heavy factions (Blood Raiders, Sansha, Amarr) and is the go-to for Paladin and Kronos pilots. Combine an Armor Repairer with damage control and hardeners for a formidable active tank.

Buffer vs. Active

Buffer tanking stacks as much EHP as possible and relies on killing enemies faster than they kill you — great for blitzing. Active tanking uses repairers that continuously repair damage — better for sustained combat against high-DPS pockets, and essential for Marauder Bastion cycles. Most endgame mission ships use active armor or shield reps inside Bastion.

Critical: Always use a Damage Control module. It provides resistance bonuses to shields, armor, and structure simultaneously and is the single best EHP module in the game slot for slot — no mission runner should undock without one.

Marauders — The Endgame

Marauders are T2 battleships built specifically for high-end PvE. Their defining feature is the Bastion Module: an active high-slot module you cycle to lock in a defensive/offensive mode. While Bastion is active you gain massive resistance bonuses, extended weapon range and rate of fire, and a +60% bonus to local armor repair and shield boost amounts (with a 20% reduction in repper cycle time and cap cost). The tradeoff: you cannot receive remote repairs or remote capacitor transfers while in Bastion.

As of the Legion Major Update (September 2025), the repair bonus was reduced from 100% to 60% but compensated with the repper efficiency bonus, keeping sustained tank strong. Marauders also lost their Micro Jump Drive cooldown reduction bonus in that update — repositioning between pockets now takes the standard 60-second MJD spool time rather than the old accelerated window. They also have built-in Tractor Beam and Salvager utilities. T2 modules are sufficient to handle any L4 mission.

Bastion Tip: Activate Bastion as soon as you're taking fire in a pocket and keep it cycling. Since the MJD cooldown bonus is gone, plan your warp-in position carefully and use the MJD to reach a good range on first land — you can still reposition between cycles but it takes a full minute each time.

Income Breakdown

Mission income comes from four stacking sources. Understanding the ratio between them lets you choose the right strategy for your goals.

Bounties 5–20M ISK Paid automatically per kill. Varies heavily by mission.
Loot & Salvage 5–25M ISK Full-clear only. Can ~double per-mission income. Skip when blitzing.
Loyalty Points (LP) 30–150M ISK value The biggest earner. Value depends heavily on faction — Sisters of EVE LP is worth 2-5× that of generic empire factions.

LP Store Strategy

Not all LP is created equal. The ISK value of Loyalty Points varies wildly between corporations. As a general rule, target at least 1,000 ISK per LP as a floor — elite choices can reach 3,000–6,000 ISK/LP.

High-Value Factions to Grind

  • Sisters of EVE (SoE): The gold standard. Their LP store sells the Astero, Stratios, and Nestor — beloved exploration and combat ships that hold strong market demand. Aim for 3,000–5,000+ ISK/LP. The catch: SoE agents are spread across low-sec and unusual locations.
  • Caldari Navy: Excellent LP store with Caldari Navy Scourge Heavy Missiles and Caldari Navy ships (Raven Navy Issue, etc.). Strong and accessible from Caldari high-sec. Typically yields 1,500–3,000 ISK/LP.
  • Ammatar Mandate / Kador / Border factions: Unique implants and faction modules that have niche but consistent market demand. Research specific items on the Fuzzwork LP Store tool before committing.
  • Generic empire factions (Federal Defense Union, State Protectorate, etc.): Faction warfare LP stores. FW missions give LP at 3–5× the rate of security missions with lower standings requirements — but they are PvP-exposed content in low-sec.
Tool: Use fuzzwork.co.uk/lpstore to check the current ISK/LP value of any faction store before grinding. The market shifts constantly — LP values change week to week based on demand for specific items.
Skill Bonus: Each level of the Security Connections skill adds +10% LP per mission from Security division agents (to a max of +50% at level V). Note: the plain "Connections" skill is a separate skill that improves effective standing, not LP — make sure you're training the right one.

Blitzing vs. Full Clear

These are two fundamentally different playstyles with different ISK sources, skill floors, and experiences.

⚡ Blitzing
Kill only the enemy ships required to trigger mission completion, warp out immediately, turn in the mission, and accept the next one as fast as possible. The majority of income comes from LP — you leave most bounties and all salvage on the table.
✔ Highest ISK/hr ceiling (200–300M+)
✔ Less time per mission pocket
✔ Far more missions completed per session
✗ Requires knowing mission trigger NPCs
✗ Less satisfying per-mission (no full clear)
✗ Standing loss risk from declining bad missions
⚔ Full Clear
Kill every NPC in the pocket, loot all wrecks, salvage with a Noctis or your Marauder's built-in salvager, collect all rewards. More ISK per individual mission, but fewer missions per hour.
✔ More ISK per mission (loot + salvage)
✔ Satisfying, thorough playstyle
✔ Good for learning mission layouts
✗ Lower ISK/hr ceiling (~120–180M)
✗ Time spent salvaging can feel slow
Hybrid approach: Many veteran mission runners blitz the mission pocket, then drop a can/bookmark and return with a dedicated Noctis salvager alt to clean up the wrecks in the background. This captures most of the ISK/hr from blitzing while recovering a significant portion of loot/salvage income.

Standings & Agent Access

Your standing determines which level of agent will give you missions. The game checks your effective standing — the highest of your standing with the specific agent, their corporation, or their faction. All three are checked against the same threshold, so reaching 5.0 with a corporation is just as valid as 5.0 with the broader faction.

Agent LevelEffective Standing RequiredNotes
Level 10.0 (Default)Available to all pilots from day one.
Level 21.0+Easy to reach after a handful of L1 missions.
Level 33.0+A few days of consistent L2 running.
Level 45.0+The main grind. Takes weeks of dedicated missioning.
Level 57.0+Low-sec content. Rarely the goal for solo high-sec runners.

Storyline Missions

Every 16th mission you complete triggers a Storyline mission from a special agent. Storyline missions give much larger faction standing gains than regular missions — they are the primary driver of faction standing improvement. Never decline or skip a storyline mission.

Declining Missions

You can decline one mission per agent every 4 hours without penalty. Declining more often causes standing loss. If you want to decline frequently (to fish for quick blitzable missions), keep your corp standing well above 5.0 as a buffer. The skill Diplomacy raises your effective faction standing, helping you access agents even when your raw faction standing has dipped.

Watch out: Declining or failing missions against an NPC corporation (e.g., running Caldari missions) also slightly reduces your standing with the opposing faction's enemies (e.g., Gallente Federation). In most cases this is minor, but it can matter for market taxes and access to other agents over time.

Pro Tips

  • Always check the mission details for the enemy faction name before undocking. Swap your hardeners and ammo to match. This takes 30 seconds and can halve your completion time.
  • Use EVE Ref (everef.net) or Pyfa for fitting validation before investing in expensive modules. Theoretical EHP and DPS numbers matter — flying blind is expensive.
  • The Micro Jump Drive is not optional on a Marauder. It lets you reposition every 60 seconds between Bastion cycles to new optimal range, turning slow battleship travel into non-issue.
  • Use the in-game Mission Journal (alt+J) to track your current standings, available storyline missions, and pending rewards from missions already completed.
  • When you reach 5.0 standing with a corporation, consider doing a Calibration Agent (Data Center) missions to boost faction standing directly — this can unlock other corps within the faction faster.
  • Keep your Cargo Capacity skill and a Mobile Tractor Unit (MTU) in your cargo. Drop the MTU at range when you land in a pocket — it pulls all wrecks to you automatically, saving enormous time when full-clearing.
  • The Fuzzwork Missioning page (fuzzwork.co.uk) contains exact mission objective data including trigger ships, warp-in distances, and alternate pockets. Bookmark it — it's indispensable for blitzing.
  • If you plan to run missions long-term, train Social V, Security Connections V, and Diplomacy IV early. The standing and LP bonuses compound over thousands of missions. Do not confuse "Connections" (standing modifier) with "Security Connections" (LP bonus) — they are different skills.
